Why Do I Code?

When I was growing up, kids didn't really have electronics like they do today. The coolest thing a kid could have was a TV in their bedroom. As most kids in the 90s, I relied on my imagination as my main source of entertainment. We could be anyone, or anything, and as video games became a a teenage staple, I began to want more out of my gaming experiences.

I wanted to have the same levels of customization as my my imagination did back then. I wanted to see strong female protagonists, kicking butt and taking names, just as good as anyone else. I almost lost my love of video games because of the sincere lack of strong female protagonists and nearly customized story archs...almost...

After long hours spent searching for something I could deem playable I stumbled upon the website of an independent video game developer who came very close to what I was looking for. One site after another, more and more independent video game developers had websites popping up left and right! However, none of them had exactly what I was looking for. As I got older, I came to a very sudden conclusion. If no one was making what I was looking for, I'd learn how to code it myself.

Learning how to code is as hard as learning a language, yet it can be as beautiful as one of Michelangelo's works of art. I enjoy the output that hours stuck behind a desk gives me. I'm enthralled with the amount of nearly limitless possibilities! I love how it makes me feel like I can develop anyone, or anything, I could dream up. I strive to one day make video games with strong female protagonists that have powerful and dynamic story archs with branching storylines that will allow players to feel capable of playing for hours! I want to create both quality video games, and quality characters.
